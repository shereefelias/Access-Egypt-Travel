## Purpose

Draft polished, brand-consistent long-form content for accessegypttravel.com. Every piece must have a scroll-stopping hook, one genuine differentiator, and a clear CTA. Never be generic.

## Step 1: Gather Input

Ask for the following before writing anything:

1. **Topic** — e.g., "Luxor temple complex," "best time to visit Egypt," "Nile cruise vs. overland tour"
2. **Content type** — blog post, destination guide, or tour description
3. **Target length** — default is 900–1,100 words for blog posts; 600–800 for destination guides; 400–600 for tour descriptions
4. **Specific angles or facts to include** — prices, guide names, itinerary details, client anecdotes, or comparison points

If the user provides enough context to start, skip straight to writing.

## Step 2: Apply Brand Voice

Write as a knowledgeable friend who has been to Egypt dozens of times — warm, specific, and reassuring. Never write like a corporate brochure.

**Voice rules:**
- Use "you" and "your" often; minimize "we" and "us"
- Be specific: "Your Egyptologist guide has 18 years of experience explaining hieroglyphics on-site" beats "our expert guides"
- Address American concerns directly: safety, USD pricing, flight logistics, time zones, what to expect
- Relate Egypt experiences to American reference points when it helps: "The scale of the Great Pyramid makes the Statue of Liberty look like a garden ornament"
- Never use these phrases unless you are deliberately subverting them: "land of the pharaohs," "timeless wonders," "once in a lifetime," "bucket list," "like stepping back in time"

**Differentiator to weave in:** Access Egypt Travel offers something large operators cannot — smaller groups or private tours, direct relationships with local Egyptologist guides, flexible itineraries, and faster personal service. Do not name competitors. Imply the contrast.

## Step 3: Structure the Content

### Blog Post Structure (800–1,200 words)
1. **Headline** — specific and SEO-friendly, targeting American search intent. Format: "[Number] Things/Reasons/Ways..." or a direct question or a bold claim with a number or price
2. **Hook intro (2–3 sentences)** — open with a scene, a surprising fact, or a direct challenge to a common fear. Do not open with "Egypt is..." or "If you're planning..."
3. **Body sections (3–5 sections)** — each with a subheading (H2), 150–200 words, one concrete fact or detail per section
4. **Practical sidebar** — at least one anchor detail: a price in USD, a specific month, a flight time, a tour duration
5. **CTA closing paragraph** — end with a direct invitation to act: "DM us on Instagram," "Email us at accessegypttravel@gmail.com," "Use the form below to start planning your trip"

### Destination Guide Structure (600–800 words)
1. **Headline** — "[Destination]: What American Travelers Actually Need to Know" or equivalent
2. **One-paragraph hook** — why this place matters AND why most travelers miss the real experience
3. **What to see** — 3–4 highlights, each with a specific detail (opening hours, best time of day, insider tip)
4. **Practical info** — how to get there, what to budget in USD, best time to visit, what to wear
5. **Access Egypt Travel angle** — one paragraph on how a private tour with a local guide changes the experience
6. **CTA** — direct and specific

### Tour Description Structure (400–600 words)
1. **Headline** — "[X] Days in Egypt: [Hook Phrase That Sells the Experience]"
2. **Selling intro (2–3 sentences)** — emotion and experience, not logistics
3. **Quick overview** — destinations, duration, group type (private or small group), price in USD
4. **3 reasons this tour is different** — bullet points, each one a genuine differentiator
5. **CTA** — email or contact form

## Step 4: SEO Considerations (Blog Posts Only)

Target a primary keyword an American would search: "Egypt tour from USA," "Is Egypt safe for Americans," "best Egypt travel company," "Nile cruise cost," "Egypt itinerary 10 days." Use the keyword naturally in the headline, first paragraph, and one subheading. Do not keyword-stuff.

## Step 5: Quality Check Before Delivering

Before presenting the draft, verify:
- [ ] Opens with a hook that is NOT a generic statement about Egypt
- [ ] Contains at least one specific price, date, or concrete stat
- [ ] Uses "you/your" at least 5 times in a blog post
- [ ] Includes at least one differentiator that a large operator cannot honestly claim
- [ ] Ends with a clear, specific CTA
- [ ] Contains none of the banned clichés (unless deliberately subverted with a twist)
- [ ] Addresses at least one American concern (safety, cost, logistics, or first-timer anxiety)

## Output Format

Deliver the draft in clean markdown with:
- H1 headline
- H2 subheadings for each section
- Bold for key facts or price anchors
- A "Suggested meta description (155 chars):" line at the bottom for blog posts

After the draft, add a single line: "Want me to adjust the tone, length, angle, or CTA?"
