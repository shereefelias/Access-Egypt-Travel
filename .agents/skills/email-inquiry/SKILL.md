## Purpose

Turn customer inquiries into booked tours by responding like a knowledgeable friend, not a sales bot. Every reply references something specific from the customer's email, answers their actual question first, and ends with one clear next step.

## Step 1: Gather Input

Ask for:
1. **The original inquiry email** — paste the full text
2. **Any known details** — travel dates, group size, budget range, special interests, physical limitations, family composition (especially children's ages)
3. **Any context** — has this person emailed before, was there a prior conversation, is there a relevant tour already in mind

If the inquiry email is pasted and contains enough detail, proceed directly.

## Step 2: Read the Inquiry Carefully

Before writing, identify:
- **The primary question** — what are they actually asking? (Price? Safety? Itinerary? Availability?)
- **The visible concern** — what anxiety is underneath the question? (Is Egypt safe? Can we handle a group? Is it worth the cost?)
- **What they told you about themselves** — first-timers? Returning travelers? Family? Couple? Interests?
- **What is missing** — travel dates, group size, budget, interests (note what to ask for)

## Step 3: Write the Email

### Opening — Never use "Thank you for your inquiry"

Open by referencing something specific from their email. This proves a human read it and sets the tone for the whole relationship.

Strong openings:
- "A 10-day trip with your two teenage kids sounds like exactly the kind of Egypt trip that sticks with a family for decades."
- "You asked about the best time to do a Nile cruise, and the honest answer depends on one thing: how much you mind heat."
- "Your question about safety is the first one most Americans ask — and it's the right one to ask before booking anything."

Weak openings (avoid):
- "Thank you for reaching out to Access Egypt Travel."
- "We received your inquiry and would be happy to help."
- "What a great question!"

### Body — Answer Their Question First

Structure:
1. **Answer their primary question directly** — 2–4 sentences, specific and honest
2. **Address their visible concern** — 1–3 sentences, reassuring without being dismissive
3. **Introduce 1–2 relevant tour options** — brief description (2–3 sentences each), price range in USD, what makes each right for their situation. Do not dump an entire catalog.
4. **Surface what you still need** — if travel dates, group size, or budget are missing, ask for them here in a natural way, not as a checklist

### Closing — One Clear Next Step

End with a single, specific action for them to take:
- "Reply with your travel dates and I'll put together a custom itinerary — no obligation, just a starting point."
- "If those dates work, I can hold a spot on our next small-group Nile cruise while we finalize details."
- "Send me your must-see list and I'll design a private route around it."

Never use: "Please don't hesitate to contact us," "Feel free to reach out," "We look forward to hearing from you" — these are filler. Replace with a specific invitation.

### Tone Rules

- Write as the owner of a small business — invested, personal, knowledgeable
- Match the customer's energy: if they're excited, be warm and excited back; if they're cautious, be measured and reassuring
- Use "I" not "we" — this email is from a person, not a company
- Specific is always better than general: "The Cairo to Luxor flight is about 1 hour and costs roughly $80–120 USD booked locally" beats "domestic travel is easy"
- Address American-specific concerns naturally: USD pricing, US hub airport connections, US time zone support, safety context

### Length

Under 350 words. Inquiry replies that run long feel like brochures. Customers respond to brevity and specificity.

### What to Address by Concern Type

| If they mention... | Address it by... |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| Safety | Brief, honest context — current travel advisory, what clients actually experience, real talk |
| First time to Egypt | Reduce intimidation — "Egypt is more manageable than most Americans expect" + one reassuring concrete fact |
| Budget / price | Give a real number or range, explain what's included, compare value (not competitor names) |
| Family with kids | Mention what ages work best, child-friendly sites, pacing options |
| Luxury expectations | Confirm high-end accommodation options exist, private guide standard |
| Limited time (7 days or fewer) | Be honest about what's realistic, offer a focused itinerary suggestion |
| Large group | Address private group pricing and logistics directly |
