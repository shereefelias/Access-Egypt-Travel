Abu Simbel Temples — Ramesses II

Two colossal temples carved into a cliff, relocated stone by stone to outwit a rising lake.

🏛️ Built ~1264 BC
📍 ~175 mi south of Aswan
☀️ Solar miracle: Feb 21 & Oct 21
🕔 5am – 6pm
Overview Gallery Good to Know Tours

Overview

Undisputedly among the world's most breathtaking monuments — two massive temples carved directly into a sandstone cliff. The Great Temple stretches about 185 feet (56 m) into the mountain and is guarded by four colossal seated statues of Ramesses II, each 66 feet high.

The solar miracle: twice a year — on February 21 and October 21 — sunlight shines straight through the entire length of the Great Temple to illuminate the inner sanctuary, where four gods sit. For the rest of the year, the sanctuary stays in darkness — a remarkable feat of ancient astronomical engineering.

Both temples were commissioned by Ramesses II, the greatest pharaoh of ancient Egypt, who ruled 67 years and built more monuments than any other. In the 1960s, under UNESCO supervision, the entire complex was cut apart and relocated to higher ground to save it from the rising waters of Lake Nasser — one of the greatest preservation feats in history. Buried under desert sand for centuries until its rediscovery in 1817, the temple's interior colors remain remarkably pristine.
