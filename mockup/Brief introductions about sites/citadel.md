# The Citadel of Saladin (Salah ad-Din)

A medieval Islamic-era fortification built by Salah ad-Din and developed by subsequent Egyptian rulers. It was the seat of government in Egypt and the residence of its rulers for nearly 700 years — from the 13th to the 19th century. The strategic hilltop location presides over the Mokattam Hills, overlooking the city and dominating its skyline.

Inside the Citadel sits the stunning Mohammed Ali Mosque (also known as the Alabaster Mosque), offering the best panoramic view of Cairo from any point in the city.

**Location:** Al-Mokattam district, Cairo.
**Hours:** Daily 8 a.m. – 4 p.m.
**Getting there:** About 30 minutes by car from the city center (traffic-dependent).
