# Esna Temple

**Built:** 180–45 BC

One of the smaller temples you'll visit — what's visible today is only the hypostyle hall, while the rest remains buried beneath the modern town of Esna. This is part of what makes it so fascinating: you can see firsthand how some 30 feet (9 meters) of Nile flood sediment accumulated over centuries, burying the ancient world beneath the modern one.

The hypostyle hall ranks among the most beautiful in all of Egypt, with exceptionally well-preserved colors — particularly striking after recent restoration work.

**Location:** About 35 miles (60 km) south of Luxor.
**Hours:** Daily 8 a.m. – 5 p.m.
**Getting there:** A few minutes' walk from the cruise ship at the Esna Lock. Also reachable by road from Luxor.
