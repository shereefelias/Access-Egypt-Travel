# The High Dam (Aswan High Dam)

**Completed:** 1971

Egypt's most important and impressive modern achievement. This engineering marvel took about 10 years to build (construction began in 1960), required 44 million cubic meters of building materials, employed around 34,000 workers, and represented a $1 billion investment.

From the top of the dam, visitors look across Lake Nasser — one of the largest man-made lakes in the world, stretching all the way into Sudan with a total length of about 340 miles (550 km). The dam controls flooding, generates hydroelectric power, supports year-round navigation on the Nile, and serves the irrigation needs of both Egypt and Sudan.
