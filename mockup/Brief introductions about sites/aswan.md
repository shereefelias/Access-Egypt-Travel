# Aswan

The southernmost city of Egypt and one of the most historically significant — it marked the southern gate of Egypt in ancient times, serving as a military base for campaigns into Nubia and the primary source of granite used for obelisks and statues throughout Egypt.

The city gained modern importance after the construction of the Aswan Dam in 1902 and even more with the High Dam in 1971. Located about 560 miles (900 km) south of Cairo, Aswan is a serene Nile valley destination where the river flows through granite rocks and around emerald islands covered in palm groves and tropical plants. Visitors enjoy sailing on a felucca around the islands, watching migrating birds glide over the water.

**Key attractions:** Philae Temple, The Unfinished Obelisk, The High Dam, Elephantine Island, the Nobles' Tombs, the Agha Khan Mausoleum, and St. Simeon's Monastery. Abu Simbel is also reached from Aswan — about 175 miles (285 km) further south.
