# Egypt at a Glance

A country in the northeast corner of Africa covering about 1 million square kilometers (385,000 sq mi). Official language: Arabic. Population: over 100 million. Islam is the main religion (~90% Sunni Muslim, ~10% Christian). The great majority of Egyptians live along the two banks of the Nile and its Delta — only about 5% of the land — while the rest is desert.

## History
With nearly 7,000 years of recorded history, Egypt's story is the longest and most fascinating in the world. The Greek historian Herodotus called it "the gift of the Nile" — the Nile was the artery of life along which the ancient Egyptians built a civilization that shaped the core of human knowledge: writing, architecture, art, agriculture, medicine, urbanization, organized religion, and centralized government. Egypt has been shaped by Greek, Persian, Roman, Arab, Ottoman, and Nubian influences, and was an early center of Christianity before being Islamized in the 7th century.

## Climate
One of the driest and sunniest countries in the world. The south (Luxor–Aswan corridor) is one of the least cloudy, least rainy regions on earth. Summer in the south (June–August) can exceed 40°C (104°F). **Best time to visit: October through April**, especially for Nile cruises and Upper Egypt sites.

## Economy
Agriculture, Suez Canal revenues, tourism, petroleum exports, natural gas, and remittances from over three million Egyptians living abroad.

## Culture
Egypt is the recognized cultural trend-setter of the Arab-speaking world — the Hollywood of Arab media, with Egyptian literature, music, film, and television heavily influencing the entire region.
