# Kom Ombo Temple (The Crocodile Temple)

**Built:** 205–180 BC

Standing right on the bank of the Nile, Kom Ombo is one of the most beautifully situated temples in Egypt. Unique in the ancient world for being dedicated to two gods simultaneously — the crocodile god Sobek and the falcon god Horus — the temple is divided symmetrically down the middle, each half dedicated to a different deity. Crocodile imagery appears everywhere on the walls. On the way out, visitors can view actual mummified crocodiles displayed in a small museum.

**Location:** About 30 miles (50 km) north of Aswan.
**Hours:** 9 a.m. – 5 p.m.
**Getting there:** A short walk from the cruise ship dock. Also reachable by road from Luxor or Aswan.
