# Philae Temple (Isis Temple)

**Built:** 690 BC

One of the most beautifully situated monuments in Egypt — built on an island in the reservoir of the Old Aswan Dam, reachable only by motor boat. Approaching the island, the scenery is breathtaking with the temple presiding over the island of Agilika (it was relocated here when the High Dam was built).

Philae was built and dedicated to the goddess Isis and was the last place where the ancient Egyptian religion was practiced, up until 550 AD. A Sound & Light show adds even more magic to the island at night.

**Location:** Agilika Island, near the Old Aswan Dam.
**Hours:** Daily 7 a.m. – 4 p.m.
**Getting there:** About 25 minutes by car from Aswan + 15 minutes by motor boat.
