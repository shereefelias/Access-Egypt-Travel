# Edfu Temple (Temple of Horus)

**Built:** 237–57 BC (Ptolemy III)

One of the most beautiful and best-preserved temples in Egypt. Edfu is the finest example of a Ptolemaic temple, dating to 237 BC. The entrance is marked by two massive pylons — towers each about 118 feet high — making for one of the most dramatic temple entrances in the ancient world.

**Location:** Halfway between Luxor and Aswan, about 70 miles (115 km) from each.
**Hours:** 6 a.m. – 5 p.m.
**Getting there:** Cruise ship passengers reach the temple by horse-drawn carriage (~15 minutes each way). Also reachable by road from Luxor or Aswan.
