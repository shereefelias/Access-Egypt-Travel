# Giza Pyramids & The Sphinx

**Built:** 2575–2465 BC

The last remaining wonder of the ancient world. About 4,000 years old, the Pyramids stand today as proof of the might, organization, and achievements of the Ancient Egyptians — leaving visitors speechless with countless unanswered questions.

The Pyramids are part of the Giza Necropolis from the 4th dynasty of the Old Kingdom. The largest belongs to King Khufu and took about 20 years to build; the other two belong to Khafre and Menkaure.

**The Sphinx** is a limestone structure — a mythical creature with a lion's body and a human head. One of the world's largest monuments: 240 feet (73 m) long and 66 feet (20 m) high. Built by King Khafre around 2600 BC.

**Location:** About 8 miles (13 km) southwest of Cairo city center.
**Hours:** Daily 8 a.m. – 5 p.m.
**Getting there:** About an hour by taxi from the city center (traffic-dependent).
