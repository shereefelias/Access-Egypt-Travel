# Abu Simbel Temples

**Built:** ~1264 BC (Ramesses II, 1279–1213 BC)

Undisputedly among the world's most breathtaking monuments. Two massive temples carved directly into a sandstone cliff — the Great Temple stretches about 185 feet (56 m) inside the mountain, guarded by four colossal seated statues of Ramesses II, each 66 feet high.

The solar miracle: twice a year — on February 21 and October 21 — sunlight shines straight through the entire length of the Great Temple to illuminate the sanctuary where four gods are seated. For the rest of the year, the sanctuary remains in darkness.

Both temples were commissioned by Ramesses II, the greatest pharaoh of ancient Egypt, who ruled for 67 years and built more monuments than any other pharaoh. In the 1960s, under UNESCO supervision, the entire complex was physically relocated to save it from the rising waters of Lake Nasser — one of the greatest preservation feats in history. The temples were rediscovered in 1817 after centuries buried under desert sand, which explains the pristine condition of the colors inside.

**Location:** About 175 miles (285 km) south of Aswan.
**Hours:** Daily 5 a.m. – 6 p.m.
**Getting there:** About 3 hours each way by road (well-paved, passing through the western Sahara). Many visitors depart very early and return by lunchtime. Flights from Aswan are also available.
