# Coptic Cairo (Old Cairo)

A part of Old Cairo believed to have been visited by the Holy Family. A fine collection of ancient churches and monasteries set within the walls of the Fortress of Babylon, built in the 6th century BC. The site includes several old churches, the Coptic Museum, a synagogue, and the Amr Ibn Al-Aas Mosque — the first mosque ever built in Egypt.

Nearby is the **National Museum of Egyptian Civilization (NMEC)**, home to the most famous royal mummies ever recovered from ancient Egypt.

**Location:** South Cairo, east bank of the Nile.
**Hours:** Daily 9 a.m. – 5 p.m.
**Getting there:** About 30 minutes by car from the city center (traffic-dependent).
