## Do Americans Need a Visa to Visit Egypt?

Yes. U.S. citizens require a visa to enter Egypt. There are three ways to get one:

1. **Egypt e-Visa online** (recommended — apply before you travel)
2. **Visa on arrival** (available at Cairo, Hurghada, and Sharm El-Sheikh airports)
3. **Egyptian embassy or consulate** in the U.S. (rarely necessary)

For most American travelers, the **e-Visa is the right choice**. It's cheaper, faster, and eliminates any risk of a long line at the airport when you arrive tired from a 12–15 hour journey.

---

## How to Get the Egypt e-Visa: Step-by-Step

### What You Need
- Valid U.S. passport with at least 6 months validity beyond your travel date
- Email address
- Credit or debit card
- Passport-style photo (digital)

### The Application

Go to the official Egypt e-Visa portal:
**https://visa2egypt.gov.eg**

Fill in your personal details, upload your photo, and pay the fee. The process takes 15–20 minutes.

**Processing time:** 3–5 business days for standard processing, though I've seen it approved in under 24 hours. Apply at least 2 weeks before travel to be safe.

**Cost:** Approximately $25 USD for a single-entry tourist visa. Prices are set by the Egyptian government and can change — verify at the portal.

**What you receive:** A PDF approval letter. Print it and bring it — Egyptian border control will ask for it alongside your passport.

---

## Visa on Arrival: Is It Still Available?

Yes, visa on arrival is still available at:
- Cairo International Airport
- Hurghada International Airport
- Sharm El-Sheikh International Airport
- Luxor International Airport

**Cost:** $25 USD (paid in U.S. dollars — bring cash).

However, I advise against relying on visa on arrival unless you've already tried the e-Visa and hit a problem. After a 12-hour flight, the last thing you want is to stand in a separate line at immigration. Arrival queues in Cairo can run 30–60 minutes during peak season.

---

## How Long Can Americans Stay in Egypt on a Tourist Visa?

A standard Egypt tourist visa allows a **30-day stay** from the date of entry, with a single entry.

If you need more time, you can extend your visa at any Passport and Immigration office in Egypt. In practice, most American tours run 7–14 days, so the 30-day limit isn't an issue.

---

## Does Your Passport Need to Be Valid for 6 Months?

Yes. Egyptian immigration requires your U.S. passport to be valid for **at least 6 months from your date of entry**. Check your passport expiration date before applying for the visa — if it's close, renew your passport first.

---

## Can Americans Visit Israel and Then Egypt?

This comes up often. Yes — Egypt and Israel have open borders, and American travelers frequently combine both countries on one trip. An Israeli stamp in your U.S. passport does not prevent entry to Egypt.

However, if you're planning to visit other Middle Eastern countries that don't recognize Israel after Egypt, those countries may refuse entry if they see an Israeli stamp. This is a separate issue from Egypt.

## Common Questions from American Travelers

### Do I need to print my e-Visa?
Yes. Print the PDF confirmation and bring it with your passport. Immigration officers will want to see the physical document.

### Can I book tours before my visa is approved?
Yes — I recommend it. Booking tours does not require a visa. Get your visa confirmed before finalizing flights, but booking the tour itself can happen at any stage.

### What if I'm arriving by cruise ship?
Cruise passengers arriving at Port Said or Alexandria typically receive a limited shore-pass visa through the cruise line, allowing visits to specific sites for the day. If you want to stay independently in Egypt outside the cruise, you'll need a standard visa. Ask your cruise line what documentation is required.

### Is there a multiple-entry visa option?
Yes — multiple-entry visas are available for $60 USD via the e-Visa portal. If you're planning to enter Egypt more than once on the same trip (for example, if you're doing Egypt + Jordan + Egypt), apply for the multiple-entry version.

I've never had a client denied entry to Egypt — but I've had a few create unnecessary stress by waiting until the week before departure.
