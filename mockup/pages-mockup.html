Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Cairo
Arrival & Welcome
Upon your arrival at Cairo International Airport (CAI), a dedicated representative will be waiting with a name sign to greet you and facilitate a seamless, private transfer to your five-star hotel. Following check-in, you will meet your private Egyptologist for a personalized orientation. This session provides an overview of your upcoming itinerary, helpful local insights, and an opportunity to address any questions. The remainder of the day is yours to relax and enjoy dinner at your leisure within the hotel. Overnight in Cairo.
Day 2
Cairo — Pharaonic
Giza Pyramids, Sphinx & Grand Egyptian Museum
We begin the day with an early departure to the Giza Plateau, allowing you to experience these iconic landmarks before the peak crowds arrive. Your expert Egyptologist will provide deep historical context, moving beyond the surface to explore the purpose and cultural significance of these architectural marvels through stories rarely captured by standard audio guides. Following a curated lunch, we will proceed to the newly opened Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) for an immersive tour of the complete Tutankhamun collection. Overnight in Cairo.
Day 3
Cairo — Islamic & Coptic
Citadel, Mohammed Ali Mosque, Coptic Cairo & NMEC — Fly to Luxor
Your day begins with a visit to the Saladin Citadel and the exquisite Mosque of Muhammad Ali, where you will enjoy the most panoramic view of Cairo available within the city. We then proceed to explore the ancient, storied streets of Coptic Cairo, home to one of the oldest Christian communities in the world. Following a curated lunch, we will visit the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization (NMEC). In the afternoon, you will be transferred to the airport for your flight to Luxor, where you will check into your hotel to rest and prepare for the adventures awaiting you the following day. Overnight in Luxor.
Day 4
Cairo → Luxor
East Bank Tour
Your journey continues with your private guide who will escort you to a highly acclaimed local restaurant for an authentic lunch. The afternoon features a tour of the monumental Karnak Temple Complex, followed by an evening visit to the Luxor Temple. Experiencing the temple illuminated at night offers a breathtaking and distinct perspective, providing a completely different atmosphere compared to a daytime visit. Overnight in Luxor.
Day 5
Luxor — West Bank Tour
Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut Temple, The Artisans' Village & Nile Felucca
Enjoy a comprehensive exploration of the West Bank, beginning with the Valley of the Kings, where you will visit three significant tombs, plus the extraordinary final resting place of Ramesses V & VI. Your tour continues with visits to the Temple of Hatshepsut, the remarkable Artisans' Village (Deir el-Medina), and the towering Colossi of Memnon. A curated lunch is included during your day of discovery. As the afternoon concludes, experience a relaxing sunset felucca ride along the Nile. Afterward, you will be transferred to Luxor Airport (LXR) with the assistance of our representative to board your flight to Cairo. Upon arrival in Cairo, our representative will meet you at the airport to facilitate a seamless transition and assist with your check-in at your airport hotel for an overnight stay. Overnight in Cairo.
Day 6
Cairo — Departure
Checkout & Final Departure
Following your checkout, our representative will collect you from your hotel and provide a private transfer to your departure terminal. To ensure a stress-free conclusion to your journey, our team will remain with you to provide full assistance with your luggage until you have successfully checked in at your airline's departure desk.

Day 2
Cairo — Pharaonic
Giza Pyramids, Sphinx & GEM
We begin the day with an early departure to the Giza Plateau, allowing you to experience these iconic landmarks before the peak crowds arrive. Your expert Egyptologist will provide deep historical context, moving beyond the surface to explore the purpose and cultural significance of these architectural marvels through stories rarely captured by standard audio guides. Cairo — Islamic, Coptic & Museum

Day 4
Aswan
Philae, High Dam & Nile Cruise Boarding
Check out of the hotel, you will embark on a memorable excursion with your tour guide to the breathtaking Philae Temple, a stunning island sanctuary dedicated to the goddess Isis that you will reach via a scenic private boat ride. After immersing yourself in the magic of this ancient wonder, you will visit the impressive Aswan High Dam and the fascinating Unfinished Obelisk. Board your Nile cruise before lunch. Enjoy dinner and your first evening on the river. Overnight in Aswan.
Day 5
Nile Cruise
Felucca Ride, Kom Ombo & Abu Simbel (add-on)
Join your private Egyptologist for a scenic felucca ride around the ancient Elephantine Island. Afternoon: Head back on board for lunch as the cruise begins sailing down the Nile. Relax on deck until we dock at Kom Ombo, where you'll take a short walk to explore the unique Kom Ombo dual Temple with your guide. Evening: Continue sailing north to Edfu, where the ship docks overnight. Overnight in Edfu.

✈️ Add-on: Abu Simbel Temples — available if time allows. Ask us when booking.
Day 6
Nile Cruise
Edfu – Esna Lock – Karnak & Luxor Temples
An early morning departure brings you and your Egyptologist guide to a waiting horse carriage (or private car), bound for the legendary Edfu Temple. Widely considered Egypt's most spectacular and intact ancient sanctuary, its soaring architecture and deeply carved relief walls bring the myth of Horus to life in vivid detail. Back on board, the cruise sails smoothly down the Nile, offering front-row seats to a timeless landscape of riverside villages, roaming farm animals, and swaying palms. The journey pauses for a fascinating crossing through the Esna Lock before continuing its scenic route to Luxor. Upon arrival, step ashore with your guide to witness the staggering scale of the Karnak Temple Complex—a world-class archaeological treasure tracing 2,000 years of royal history. As the sun sets, visit the magnificent Luxor Temple and stroll down the legendary Avenue of Sphinxes. Experiencing these monuments illuminated against the night sky offers a completely different, awe-inspiring atmosphere than a daytime visit. Overnight in Luxor.
Day 7
Luxor — West Bank Tour
Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut, The Artisans' Village — Fly to Cairo
Check out of the cruise, then head to the west bank of the Nile, enjoy a comprehensive exploration of the West Bank, beginning with the Valley of the Kings, where you will visit three significant tombs, plus the extraordinary tomb of Ramesses V & VI. Your tour continues with visits to the Temple of Hatshepsut, the remarkable Artisans' Village (Deir el-Medina), and the towering Colossi of Memnon. A curated lunch is included at a highly-rated local restaurant during your day of discovery. Finally, you will be comfortably transferred to Luxor Airport (LXR) to catch your flight back to Cairo (an hour flight) where our representatives will pick you up and transfer you to your airport hotel; check in and enjoy a peaceful night sleep. Overnight in Cairo.
Day 8
Cairo
Final Departure
Our company representative will meet you at your hotel to transfer you to the departure terminal, providing full luggage assistance until you are safely checked in at the airline desk.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Cairo
Arrival & Welcome
Your dedicated representative will meet you at Cairo International Airport (CAI) with a name sign. Private transfer to your 5-star hotel. Check-in and meet your Egyptologist guide for a brief orientation — what to expect, tips for the days ahead, and any questions answered. Rest of the day free. Dinner on your own at the hotel. Overnight in Cairo.
Day 2
Cairo — Pharaonic
Giza Pyramids, the Sphinx & The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)
We begin the day with an early departure to the Giza Plateau, allowing you to experience these iconic landmarks before the peak crowds arrive. Your expert Egyptologist will provide deep historical context, moving beyond the surface to explore the purpose and cultural significance of these architectural marvels through stories rarely captured by standard audio guides. Following a curated lunch, we will proceed to the newly opened Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) for an immersive tour of the complete Tutankhamun collection. Overnight in Cairo.
Day 3
Cairo — Islamic & Coptic — Museum
Citadel, Mohammed Ali Mosque, Coptic Cairo & NMEC — Fly to Luxor
Your day begins with a visit to the Saladin Citadel and the exquisite Mosque of Muhammad Ali, where you will enjoy the most panoramic view of Cairo available within the city. We then proceed to explore the ancient, storied streets of Coptic Cairo, home to one of the oldest Christian communities in the world. Following a curated lunch, we will visit the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization (NMEC); final home of the most famous royal mummies. In the afternoon, you will be transferred to Cairo airport for your flight to Luxor (an hour flight) where you will be assisted by our representative until you check into your hotel to rest and prepare for the adventures awaiting you the following day. Overnight in Luxor.
Day 4
Luxor — East Bank Tour
Karnak Temples, Luxor Temple & The Sphinx Avenue — Nile Cruise Boarding
Have breakfast then check out of your hotel and join your tour guide to visit Karnak Temples; the largest monumental complex in the world which is known as the history book of Ancient Egypt then head to your cruise ship for check in and lunch. Bypassing Luxor Temple daytime deliberately because it won't be just the history but we invite you to experience Luxor Temple and The Sphinx Avenue bathed in the golden glow of twilight; a totally extraordinary experience. Dinner on the ship. Overnight in Luxor.
Day 5
Luxor — West Bank Tour
The Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut Temple & The Village of the Artisans
Early breakfast on the ship then join your tour guide to the heart of the mountains of western Thebes where Egypt's best secret depicting the afterlife of the Pharaohs with dazzling, untouched colors and myths that refuse to fade. You will visit 3 royal tombs plus the extraordinary tomb of Ramesses V & VI. Followed by Hatshepsut's Temple rising majestically out of the desert cliffs; a breathtaking triumph of ancient architecture where the most powerful lady of Ancient Egypt's history built her mortuary temple. Our third site takes you off the Pharaohs' track into the ancient world's ultimate artisan village; this is ancient Egypt at its most human where you can see the ruins of their village and intact tombs. Finally, we will stop at the Colossi of Memnon on our way back to the cruise ship. Back on board, lunch, the cruise sails smoothly down the Nile, offering front-row seats to a timeless landscape of riverside villages, roaming farm animals, and swaying palms. The journey pauses for a fascinating crossing through the Esna Lock until the ship docks at Edfu. Overnight in Edfu.
Day 6
Nile Cruise
Edfu & Kom Ombo Temples
Breakfast on board, then join your tour guide to visit Egypt's most preserved ancient sanctuary where time has stood completely still for over two millennia; walk through the massive gateways, towering roofs of the falcon headed deity Horus of Edfu. Back to the boat, lunch while sailing with the marvelous Nile scenery around. Docking at Kom Ombo in the afternoon, then your tour guide will accompany you to visit Egypt's only twin temple shadowed by the sunlight that was dedicated to two deities. Continue sailing until you dock in Aswan. Dinner on board. Overnight in Aswan.
Day 7
Aswan
Philae Temple, The Unfinished Obelisk, High Dam & Nubian Village
Breakfast on board, then join your tour guide for a visit to the pearl of Egypt where you get on a boat and cross over to the floating sanctuary of Isis, surrounded by water on all sides feels like stepping into a living postcard. Followed by The High Dam; a rock-filled dam overlooking Lake Nasser; the largest manmade lake in the world. Finally, we will visit the ancient quarry of granite where the Unfinished Obelisk is still laying; the largest and heaviest in the world. Lunch on board, then a special visit by a motorboat to the Nubian Village; a photographer's dream, with its tiny streets, Nubian people and rainbow-painted house and geometrical designs; it's a visual feast for the eyes. Dinner on board. Overnight in Aswan.
Day 8
Aswan → Cairo
Fly to Cairo (optional Abu Simbel excursion)
Check out of the cruise ship and enjoy a hassle-free transfer to Aswan Airport (ASW) with our representative handling your luggage. Lunch is included. Arrive in Cairo for a seamless pickup and private transfer directly to your airport hotel. Overnight in Cairo.
Day 9
Cairo
Final Departure
Breakfast at the hotel, check out. Our company representative will meet you at your hotel to transfer you to the departure terminal, providing full luggage assistance until you are safely checked in at the airline desk.

Aswan

🛡️ Safety & Travel Conditions
Is Egypt safe for American tourists right now?
Egypt's main tourist sites — Cairo, Luxor, Aswan, Hurghada — are safe for American travelers and have been for many years. The Egyptian government prioritizes tourism security heavily. You'll notice tourist police at major sites, dedicated tourist lanes, and well-established infrastructure for foreign visitors. We monitor conditions daily and will always tell you honestly if anything has changed. The US State Department currently rates Egypt as Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution) — the same rating as France, Germany, and much of Europe, primarily due to isolated incidents nowhere near tourist areas.
Is it safe to travel alone as a woman in Egypt?
With a private guide and structured itinerary, solo female travel in Egypt is very manageable. Having a private guide rather than joining a group tour makes a significant difference — you're not navigating public spaces alone. We recommend modest dress at religious sites (we'll tell you exactly what to wear). Most of our solo female travelers report feeling safe throughout. If you have specific concerns, contact us and we'll give you honest, specific advice for your itinerary.
What's the best time of year to visit Egypt?
October through April is ideal for most travelers — mild temperatures, manageable humidity, and full access to outdoor sites. Summer (June–August) in Luxor can reach 110°F (43°C), which is brutal for extended outdoor touring. We run tours year-round but strongly advise most American visitors to avoid July–August unless they're heat-tolerant. November, March, and April are our most popular months — if you're targeting spring break or Thanksgiving, book early.
📍 Pickup Locations & Logistics
Do you pick up from Hurghada?
Yes — this is one of our most popular options. We offer hotel pickups from Hurghada, and transfer to Luxor is included in your package. The drive takes approximately 3 hours through the Eastern Desert. Many American travelers book a Red Sea resort stay first and then join us for Luxor — it's one of the most efficient ways to combine beach and ancient history in a single Egypt trip. Pickup times are arranged based on your tour schedule and hotel location.
Can I join a tour from Cairo?
Absolutely. We offer hotel pickups from Cairo. For the Cairo-to-Luxor transfer, you have two options: (1) Domestic flight — EgyptAir Cairo to Luxor, approximately 1 hour, typically $60–100 USD — this is what we recommend for most travelers. (2) Road transfer — approximately 8–10 hours through the Nile Valley. Most guests prefer the flight. We can arrange everything; just let us know your preference when booking.
What airport should I fly into?
It depends on your itinerary. Most American travelers fly into Cairo International Airport (CAI) — it has the most direct US connections (usually via European hubs like Amsterdam, London, Frankfurt, or Doha). If you're starting with a Red Sea stay, Hurghada International Airport (HRG) is your entry point. We'll advise you on the best arrival/departure points for your specific package when you contact us.
🛂 Visas & Entry
Do Americans need a visa for Egypt?
Yes, but it's simple. American citizens can get a visa on arrival at Cairo, Hurghada, and Luxor airports — it costs $25 USD (pay in cash). Alternatively, you can apply online for an e-Visa before travel at visa2egypt.gov.eg — also $25. We recommend the e-Visa to avoid lines on arrival. The visa is valid for 30 days. We include a visa assistance service for all clients — we'll guide you through the exact steps before you travel.
Is my US passport sufficient? Any restrictions?
Your US passport is all you need. Egypt has no restrictions on American passport holders beyond the standard visa requirement. Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months from your arrival date — standard for international travel. That's it. We'll confirm all entry requirements for your travel date when you book.

That's exactly what we do. Every tour we run is private and built around your interests and schedule. The packages on our website are starting points — not fixed itineraries. Want more time at the Valley of the Kings? Want to skip the sound and light show and do a sunset felucca instead? Tell us. We build your trip around what matters to you.
🎒 What to Expect on Tour
What should I pack for Egypt?
Essentials: Lightweight, breathable clothing (avoid synthetic fabrics in heat). For religious sites (mosques, churches): shoulders and knees covered — scarves/shawls work. Shoes: Comfortable closed-toe walking shoes — you'll be on sand, stone, and uneven surfaces. Sun protection: High-SPF sunscreen, hat, and UV-blocking sunglasses — non-negotiable from March onwards. Cash: Small US dollars or Egyptian pounds for tips, market purchases, and optional extras. We'll send you a full packing list when you book.
What food and drinks should I know about?
Egyptian food is excellent — and your included lunches will introduce you to it properly. Don't drink tap water — bottled water is provided during excursions and is cheap and readily available everywhere. Stick to bottled or filtered water for brushing teeth too if your stomach is sensitive. Most travelers have no issues. Dietary restrictions: Let us know in advance and we'll ensure your included meals are appropriate.
How much cash should I bring?
For a 6-day tour, we suggest budgeting roughly $200–300 USD cash for tips, optional extras, personal shopping, and meals not included (dinners). Your guide will give you a realistic breakdown for your specific itinerary. US dollars are accepted widely at tourist sites; Egyptian pounds are useful for smaller purchases. We'll advise you on the current exchange rate before your trip.
