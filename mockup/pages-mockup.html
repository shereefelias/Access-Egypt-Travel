Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Cairo
Arrival & Welcome
Your dedicated representative meets you at Cairo International Airport with a nameplate. Private transfer to your 5-star hotel. Check-in, settle in, and meet your Egyptologist guide for a brief orientation — what to expect, tips for the days ahead, and any questions answered. Rest of the day free. Dinner on your own.
Day 2
Cairo — Pharaonic
Giza Pyramids, Sphinx & Grand Egyptian Museum
An early start to beat the crowds at the Giza Plateau. Your guide explains not just what you're looking at but why it was built, and by whom — stories no audio guide can tell. Lunch included. Afternoon: the Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM), newly fully open — home to the complete Tutankhamun collection.
Day 3
Cairo — Islamic & Coptic
Citadel, Mohammed Ali Mosque & Coptic Cairo
Saladin Citadel and the beautiful Mohammed Ali Mosque — the best view of Cairo from any point in the city. Then the ancient churches and streets of Coptic Cairo, one of the oldest Christian communities in the world. National Museum of Egyptian Civilization (NMEC). Lunch included.
Day 4
Cairo → Luxor
Travel Day + East Bank Arrival
Transfer to Luxor — by domestic flight (EgyptAir, ~1 hour) or overnight train (your choice; see pricing). Check in to your Luxor hotel. Evening: Karnak Temples and Luxor Temple lit up at sunset — a completely different experience from the daytime.
Day 5
Luxor — West Bank
Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut Temple & Nile Felucca
The full West Bank experience: Valley of the Kings (tombs of Ramesses II, Tutankhamun, and others), Temple of Hatshepsut, the artisans' village at Deir el-Medina, and the Colossi of Memnon. Lunch included. Sunset felucca ride on the Nile — the perfect end to your Egypt days.
Day 6
Luxor — Departure
Checkout & Farewell
Hotel checkout at leisure. Private transfer to Luxor Airport for your onward flight home or to your next destination. Your guide will be with you until you're checked in and through security.

🛡️ Safety & Travel Conditions
Is Egypt safe for American tourists right now?
Egypt's main tourist sites — Cairo, Luxor, Aswan, Hurghada — are safe for American travelers and have been for many years. The Egyptian government prioritizes tourism security heavily. You'll notice tourist police at major sites, dedicated tourist lanes, and well-established infrastructure for foreign visitors. We monitor conditions daily and will always tell you honestly if anything has changed. The US State Department currently rates Egypt as Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution) — the same rating as France, Germany, and much of Europe, primarily due to isolated incidents nowhere near tourist areas.
Is it safe to travel alone as a woman in Egypt?
With a private guide and structured itinerary, solo female travel in Egypt is very manageable. Having a private guide rather than joining a group tour makes a significant difference — you're not navigating public spaces alone. We recommend modest dress at religious sites (we'll tell you exactly what to wear). Most of our solo female travelers report feeling safe throughout. If you have specific concerns, contact us and we'll give you honest, specific advice for your itinerary.
What's the best time of year to visit Egypt?
October through April is ideal for most travelers — mild temperatures, manageable humidity, and full access to outdoor sites. Summer (June–August) in Luxor can reach 110°F (43°C), which is brutal for extended outdoor touring. We run tours year-round but strongly advise most American visitors to avoid July–August unless they're heat-tolerant. November, March, and April are our most popular months — if you're targeting spring break or Thanksgiving, book early.
📍 Pickup Locations & Logistics
Do you pick up from Hurghada?
Yes — this is one of our most popular options. We offer hotel pickups from Hurghada, and transfer to Luxor is included in your package. The drive takes approximately 3 hours through the Eastern Desert. Many American travelers book a Red Sea resort stay first and then join us for Luxor — it's one of the most efficient ways to combine beach and ancient history in a single Egypt trip. Pickup times are arranged based on your tour schedule and hotel location.
Can I join a tour from Cairo?
Absolutely. We offer hotel pickups from Cairo. For the Cairo-to-Luxor transfer, you have two options: (1) Domestic flight — EgyptAir Cairo to Luxor, approximately 1 hour, typically $60–100 USD — this is what we recommend for most travelers. (2) Road transfer — approximately 8–10 hours through the Nile Valley. Most guests prefer the flight. We can arrange everything; just let us know your preference when booking.
What airport should I fly into?
It depends on your itinerary. Most American travelers fly into Cairo International Airport (CAI) — it has the most direct US connections (usually via European hubs like Amsterdam, London, Frankfurt, or Doha). If you're starting with a Red Sea stay, Hurghada International Airport (HRG) is your entry point. We'll advise you on the best arrival/departure points for your specific package when you contact us.
🛂 Visas & Entry
Do Americans need a visa for Egypt?
Yes, but it's simple. American citizens can get a visa on arrival at Cairo, Hurghada, and Luxor airports — it costs $25 USD (pay in cash). Alternatively, you can apply online for an e-Visa before travel at visa2egypt.gov.eg — also $25. We recommend the e-Visa to avoid lines on arrival. The visa is valid for 30 days. We include a visa assistance service for all clients — we'll guide you through the exact steps before you travel.
Is my US passport sufficient? Any restrictions?
Your US passport is all you need. Egypt has no restrictions on American passport holders beyond the standard visa requirement. Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months from your arrival date — standard for international travel. That's it. We'll confirm all entry requirements for your travel date when you book.

🎒 What to Expect on Tour
What should I pack for Egypt?
Essentials: Lightweight, breathable clothing (avoid synthetic fabrics in heat). For religious sites (mosques, churches): shoulders and knees covered — scarves/shawls work. Shoes: Comfortable closed-toe walking shoes — you'll be on sand, stone, and uneven surfaces. Sun protection: High-SPF sunscreen, hat, and UV-blocking sunglasses — non-negotiable from March onwards. Cash: Small US dollars or Egyptian pounds for tips, market purchases, and optional extras. We'll send you a full packing list when you book.
What food and drinks should I know about?
Egyptian food is excellent — and your included lunches will introduce you to it properly. Don't drink tap water — bottled water is provided during all excursions and is cheap and readily available everywhere. Stick to bottled or filtered water for brushing teeth too if your stomach is sensitive. Most travelers have no issues. Dietary restrictions: Let us know in advance and we'll ensure your included meals are appropriate.
How much cash should I bring?
For a 6-day tour, we suggest budgeting roughly $200–300 USD cash for tips, optional extras, personal shopping, and meals not included (dinners). Your guide will give you a realistic breakdown for your specific itinerary. US dollars are accepted widely at tourist sites; Egyptian pounds are useful for smaller purchases. We'll advise you on the current exchange rate before your trip.
