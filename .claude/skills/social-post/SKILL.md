## Purpose

Write scroll-stopping social media content for Access Egypt Travel that feels personal and expert, not corporate. Every post has a hook, one real differentiator, and a CTA. Facebook and Instagram have different formats — follow each carefully.

## Step 1: Gather Input

Ask for:
1. **Platform** — Facebook or Instagram (or both)
2. **Topic or photo description** — what is happening in the image or what is the post about
3. **Goal** — traffic (click to website), engagement (comments/shares), brand awareness, or sales (tour inquiry)

If the user gives enough detail to proceed, skip the questions and write.

## Step 2: Write the Hook First

The first sentence is the only sentence guaranteed to be read. It must:
- Stop the scroll
- Raise a question, challenge a fear, or make a bold specific claim
- Never start with "We" or "Access Egypt Travel" or "Egypt is"
- Never start with a cliché

Strong hook examples:
- "Most Americans fly 14 hours to Egypt and miss the best part."
- "Your Cairo hotel is 20 minutes from the Pyramids. You don't need a 10-day group tour to see them."
- "An Egyptologist just explained something to a client that her $6,000 group tour guide never mentioned."
- "The Nile at sunrise looks exactly like you imagine it. The price doesn't."

Weak hook examples (avoid):
- "Egypt is an incredible destination..."
- "We are so excited to share..."
- "Have you ever dreamed of..."

## Step 3: Write the Post Body

### Facebook Format

- **Total length:** 100–300 words
- **Tone:** Conversational, can tell a short story or share a behind-the-scenes moment
- **Structure:** Hook sentence → 2–4 sentences of body (story, fact, or tip) → 1 sentence on what Access Egypt Travel offers differently → CTA
- **Links:** Include the website URL (accessegypttravel.com) or a specific tour page when the goal is traffic
- **Emojis:** 1–2 maximum, used purposefully — not decoratively
- **Engagement:** End with a question to invite comments: "Have you been to Egypt? Tell us what you loved most." or "What's the one thing stopping you from booking? Drop it in the comments."
- **No hashtags** on Facebook — they perform poorly there

Facebook template:
```
[Hook sentence]

[2–3 sentences: the story, the fact, the experience — specific and warm]

[1 sentence: what you get with Access Egypt Travel that a big operator can't give you]

[CTA] → [URL if traffic goal]

[Engagement question]
```

### Instagram Format

- **Total length:** 50–150 words
- **First line:** The hook — must work as a standalone sentence visible before "more" is tapped
- **Tone:** Visual-first. Describe what the viewer is seeing if relevant: "This is the Karnak temple complex at the moment it opens to the public — before the crowds."
- **Body:** 2–4 short sentences. Use line breaks between thoughts (Instagram reads better with white space)
- **CTA:** "Link in bio," "DM us to start planning," "Drop your questions below"
- **Emojis:** Optional, 1–3 max if used — only where they genuinely add tone
- **Hashtags:** 3–5 quality hashtags at the end, on a new line

Instagram template:
```
[Hook sentence — must work standalone before "more"]

[1–2 sentences describing the experience or scene]

[1 sentence on the Access Egypt Travel difference]

[CTA]

[hashtag1] [hashtag2] [hashtag3]
```

## Step 4: Apply Brand Voice Throughout

- Use "you" and "your" — not "travelers" or "guests"
- Be warm but specific — not corporate or generic
- Weave in one real differentiator: private guides, Egyptologist expertise, small groups, flexibility, personal service, USD pricing transparency
- Address American concerns naturally: safety, cost, logistics — do not ignore them, do not lecture about them
- Never name competitors

## Step 5: Hashtag Selection (Instagram Only)

Provide 3 options in 3 tiers:

1. **Broad reach (1):** #EgyptTravel or #VisitEgypt — high volume, competitive
2. **Mid-tier (2):** #EgyptTourism #NileCruise #AncientEgypt #LuxorEgypt #CairoEgypt — pick 2 relevant to the post topic
3. **Niche/conversion (2):** #EgyptTourOperator #BoutiqueTravel #PrivateEgyptTour #AmericansInEgypt — pick 1–2

Present the final 3–5 as a ready-to-paste block.
