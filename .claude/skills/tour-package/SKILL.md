## Purpose

Produce complete, WordPress-ready tour package pages that sell the experience before they list the logistics. Every package page must feel personal and specific — not like a catalog entry from a large operator.

## Step 1: Gather Input

Ask for the following. Do not write the page until you have the essentials (starred items):

- **Package name*** — working title or final name
- **Duration*** — number of days
- **Destinations covered*** — cities, sites, regions
- **Price per person (USD)*** — be specific; if there are group vs. private rates, get both
- **Group type*** — private tour, small group (specify max size), or both options
- **Highlights** — 3–5 signature experiences or moments
- **Known inclusions** — accommodation, meals (which ones), domestic transport, guide type, entrance fees
- **Known exclusions** — anything the customer pays separately
- **Audience** — families, couples, solo travelers, adventure seekers, luxury, budget?
- **Any existing tour page or notes** — if updating an existing listing, get the current content

## Step 2: Write the Headline

Format: **"[X] Days in Egypt: [Hook Phrase That Sells the Experience"**

The hook phrase must sell the feeling, not describe the logistics.

Strong examples:
- "12 Days in Egypt: The Nile, the Pharaohs, and No Crowds"
- "8 Days in Egypt: Private Access, Expert Guides, All-Inclusive From $2,495"
- "10 Days in Egypt: From the Pyramids to Abu Simbel With Someone Who Knows Every Story"

Weak examples (avoid):
- "10-Day Egypt Tour Package"
- "Classic Egypt Itinerary"
- "Egypt Discovery Tour"

## Step 3: Write the Intro Paragraph

2–3 sentences. Sell the experience and the feeling — not the itinerary. The logistics come later. This paragraph is what makes someone keep reading.

Include:
- What this trip feels like from the traveler's perspective
- One emotional payoff (awe, discovery, relief at how smooth it is, the intimacy of a private guide)
- One specific detail that anchors it as real (a price, a site name, a guide credential)

Do not open with "This tour..." or "Join us..." or "Egypt has..."

## Step 4: Write the Itinerary

Day-by-day breakdown. Use this structure for each day:

**Day [X]: [Location] — [Theme or Highlight]**
Morning: [Activity + specific detail]
Afternoon: [Activity + specific detail]
Evening: [Activity or accommodation note]
*Overnight: [Hotel name or category — e.g., "5-star Nile-view hotel in Luxor"]*

Rules:
- Every day entry must have at least one specific detail (a site name, a guide action, a local experience)
- Do not write "free time" without offering a suggestion
- Note meals included for each day (e.g., "Breakfast and dinner included")
- Flag any domestic flights or overnight trains

## Step 5: Write the Inclusions Table

Use two columns. Be specific — vague inclusions lose trust.

| Included | Not Included |
|----------|-------------|
| Airport transfers on arrival and departure | International flights to/from Cairo |
| All domestic transport within the itinerary | Travel insurance (required — we recommend World Nomads) |
| [X]-night accommodation ([Y]-star level) | Tips for guides and drivers (suggested: $10–15/day/guide) |
| Daily breakfast; [other meals] | Personal expenses and shopping |
| Private Egyptologist guide throughout | Optional upgrades or extensions |
| All entrance fees listed in the itinerary | Visa on arrival fee ($25 USD at Cairo airport) |
| 24/7 on-trip support via WhatsApp | |

Adjust based on what the user provides. Always include the visa note — Americans need it and often don't know.

## Step 6: Write the Price Block

Format clearly for American readers:

**Tour Price**
- Private tour (up to [X] people): **$[price] per person**
- Small group (max [X] people): **$[price] per person**

*All prices in USD. Prices based on double occupancy. Single supplement: +$[amount].*

*International flights not included. Most clients fly from New York (JFK), Los Angeles (LAX), Chicago (ORD), or Washington D.C. (IAD) with a connection in Europe. Average round-trip flight cost: $900–$1,400 USD.*

## Step 7: Write the FAQ Section

Write 4–5 Q&As specific to this package. Do not use generic FAQs. Base them on what American first-timers actually ask.

For each FAQ:
- Start with the question an anxious traveler would actually type
- Answer honestly and specifically — 2–4 sentences
- Address: safety, physical requirements, best time to go, what to wear, tipping, visa, payment

Example questions:
- "Is Egypt safe for American tourists right now?"
- "How much walking is involved in this itinerary?"
- "Can I extend this trip to add [destination]?"
- "What do I need to know about the visa?"
- "What's the best time of year for this itinerary?"

## Step 8: Write the CTA Block

End the page with:

**Ready to Book?**

Contact us at **accessegypttravel@gmail.com** or use the form below. Tell us your travel dates and group size and we'll confirm availability and send a detailed proposal — no commitment required.

Private tours can be customized around your schedule. Small group dates are set — reply to see the next available departure.

## Step 9: Weave In Differentiators Throughout

Integrate these naturally — not as a bullet-pointed boast section:

- **Private Egyptologist guides** — name the credential, explain what it means in practice
- **Small group sizes** — what is the max? Why does it matter at these sites?
- **Flexibility** — can the itinerary be adjusted? Can they add a day?
- **Personal service** — WhatsApp support, direct owner contact, US time zone availability
- **Transparent USD pricing** — no currency surprises, no hidden fees
- **American framing** — mention US hub airports, approximate flight times, what to expect at Cairo airport

Do not name competitors. Let the differentiators speak through specificity.
