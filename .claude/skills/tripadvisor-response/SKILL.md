## Purpose

Write TripAdvisor responses that serve two audiences simultaneously: the reviewer (acknowledged and respected) and the prospective traveler reading the exchange (reassured and persuaded). Every response is a marketing opportunity. Never waste it with generic or defensive language.

## Step 1: Gather Input

Ask for:
1. **The review text** — paste the full review
2. **Star rating** — 1 through 5
3. **Any context about the trip or issue** — dates, what happened, any prior communication, how it was resolved

If the user pastes the review and rating, proceed directly.

## Step 2: Identify Response Type

- **4–5 stars:** Positive response (see Step 3)
- **1–3 stars:** Negative/critical response (see Step 4)
- **3 stars with mixed feedback:** Use positive framework but acknowledge the specific friction point honestly

## Step 3: Positive Review Response (4–5 Stars)

**Goal:** Thank authentically, prove you read it, add marketing value for prospective readers, invite a return.

**Rules:**
- Open with the reviewer's name if it appears in the review — "Thank you, Sarah!" not "Dear Valued Customer"
- Echo one specific detail from the review in the first 2 sentences — this proves the response is genuine
- Add one piece of information useful to prospective travelers: a fact about guide expertise, a detail about how tours are structured, a service feature. This is your free marketing sentence.
- End with an invitation: to return, to share the experience with friends, or to reach out when planning the next trip
- **Length:** Under 150 words — tight responses feel more human
- **Tone:** Warm, genuine, proud without being boastful

**Do not use:**
- "We're so glad you enjoyed your experience"
- "Thank you for choosing us"
- "We strive to provide excellent service"
- Any phrase that could have been auto-generated

**Template structure:**
```
[Name], [echo a specific detail from their review in a genuine way].

[One sentence adding value for prospective readers — guide expertise, tour structure, what makes the experience different].

[Closing invitation — return, share, or reach out for next trip].

[Sign-off]
```

**Example:**
"Sarah, hearing that your Egyptologist guide brought the hieroglyphics at Karnak to life in a way you hadn't expected — that's exactly what we set out to do. Our guides average over 15 years working these sites, and they love going beyond the standard tour script. We hope Egypt has found a permanent place on your travel list. When you're ready to come back — and most people want to — we'll be here. The Access Egypt Travel Team"

## Step 4: Negative Review Response (1–3 Stars)

**Goal:** Acknowledge specifically, take accountability or clarify fairly, offer resolution, reassure prospective readers that this is not the norm.

**The prospective traveler rule:** Write every sentence as if a potential customer is deciding whether to book based on how you handle this. Defensive responses lose bookings. Calm, accountable, solution-oriented responses often win them.

**Rules:**
- Open with thanks — genuine, not sycophantic
- Name the specific issue from the review — never respond to the general sentiment without addressing the specific complaint
- Do not say "We're sorry for the inconvenience" — it reads as dismissive and robotic
- Do not be defensive — even if the review contains inaccuracies, stay calm and factual
- Explain what was done, what will be done, or what the correct information is — concisely
- Invite offline resolution: direct them to accessegypttravel@gmail.com
- **Length:** Under 200 words
- **Tone:** Professional, calm, and genuinely concerned — not apologetic to the point of groveling

**Do not use:**
- "We're sorry for the inconvenience"
- "We take all feedback seriously" (implies you don't always)
- "This is not the experience we aim to provide" (without specifics)
- Anything that sounds like it was copied from a complaint-handling manual

**Template structure:**
```
[Thank them for the feedback — specific to what they took time to write].

[Acknowledge the specific issue directly — name it, don't dance around it].

[Explain what happened, what was done, or what you are doing differently — one to two sentences, factual and fair].

[Invite offline resolution]: Please reach out directly at accessegypttravel@gmail.com so we can make this right.

[Brief reassurance for prospective travelers — one sentence about your standard].

[Sign-off]
```

## Step 5: Sign-Off

- If the owner's name is known: "Warmly, [Owner Name] — Access Egypt Travel"
- If not: "The Access Egypt Travel Team"
- Never sign as "Management" — it sounds corporate

## Output Format

Deliver the response as clean, copy-paste ready text with no markdown formatting (TripAdvisor is plain text):

**TRIPADVISOR RESPONSE — [POSITIVE/NEGATIVE] — [X] stars:**
[Response text, plain text, no bullet points, no bold, no markdown]
Word count: [X]

After the response, add: "Want me to adjust the tone, add/remove the marketing line, or try a different opening?"
